Multiple Linear Regression

To find the best multiple linear model, we begin with our full model selected from our EDA. We make the following assumptions for our models:

  1. Linearity: The relationship between predictors and the outcome is linear.
  2. Independence: Observations (students) are independent.
  3. Homoscedasticity: The variance of error terms is constant across groups.
  4. Normality: The residuals are approximately normally distributed.
full_model <- lm(score ~ class_type+subject+gender+race+grade+urban, df_subset)

Mixed Models

We make the following assumptions for our mixed models:

  1. Linearity: The relationship between predictors and the outcome is linear.
  2. Independence: Observations (students) are independent.
  3. Homoscedasticity: The variance of error terms is constant across groups.
  4. Normality: The residuals are approximately normally distributed. Stratifying using schid: Grouping students by schools.
mixed_model_sch <- lmer(score
    ~ class_type
    + subject
    + gender
    + race
    + grade
    + subject*grade
    + (1|schid),
    data=df_subset)
